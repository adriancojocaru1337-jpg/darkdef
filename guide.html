Getting Started

The basics

Dark Defense is a tower defense game: enemies walk a fixed path toward your gate, and you place towers alongside it to destroy them before they cross. Each kill earns gold; each leak costs a life. Survive every wave in a stage to win — or push as far as you can in Endless.

  • Place towers on buildable ground next to the path. Tap a spot, pick a tower, confirm.
  • Earn gold from kills and wave clears; spend it on new towers or upgrades.
  • Start each wave with the Start button — tap once for a single wave, or double-tap it to toggle Auto (waves start on their own).
  • Upgrade your best towers instead of spreading gold thin — a well-placed upgraded tower beats several weak ones.
  • Protect your lives: a flawless wave earns bonus score, and lives carry your run's momentum.
Pausing freezes the field — you can inspect towers, but you can't place new ones until you resume.

The Six Bosses

Permanent Progression

Ascension & Crystals

Every run — win or lose — earns Crystals. Spend them in Ascension on permanent upgrades that carry into every future run, so you grow stronger the more you play. Progress saves locally and syncs to your account when signed in.

  • 🔆 Radiance — raw firepower: more tower damage, faster attacks, extra range, empowered ley stones.
  • 🛡️ Bastion — economy and survival: bonus starting gold and lives, more gold per kill, stronger stage-clear healing.
  • 🔮 Arcana — spells and drafts: shorter spell cooldowns, aura rerolls, more Crystals earned, wider boss aura choices.
  • ❄ Special Towers — unlock Cryo (150 Crystals), then its four upgrades: Deep Frost (stronger chill), Glacial Reach (range & field), Frostbite (damage), Shatterpoint (extra Brittle) — each with four ranks at 300 / 600 / 1000 / 1600.
Node costs rise with each rank, so early attunements are cheap catch-up power, while the deepest upgrades — especially the Cryo tree — are long-term goals worth grinding toward.
Survival Mode

Endless mode

After you clear the campaign, Endless pits you against unlimited waves that scale forever. It's where high scores and leaderboard runs are made.

  • Doubled enemy health — every non-boss mob has 2× HP, and health keeps rising each wave.
  • Boss pairs with a horde — every tenth wave sends two bosses, now preceded by a full wave of mobs, not just the pair alone.
  • Rotating soundtrack — after each boss pair falls, the music rerolls to a random stage theme.
  • Fewer Crystals than campaign — Endless rewards are deliberately leaner, so it's about the score, not the farm.
  • Leaderboard runs — reach as high a wave as you can; your bonus score is submitted globally at game over.
Play Like a High-Score Run

Advanced strategy

Positioning — use route geometry, not random placement

The strongest layouts are built around repeated exposure. Place towers where enemies stay in range longest — around turns, along long lanes, and in overlapping coverage pockets. A mediocre tower in the right place usually beats a better tower placed badly. Let splash towers soften groups while longer-range towers finish priority targets before they cross the final third of the map.

Boss prep — plan waves before they start

  • Save gold before boss waves instead of making weak filler buys.
  • Favor high single-target uptime for bosses (Hunter → Sniper, upgraded Bomb).
  • Keep at least one spell ready as insurance against a surge or shield drop.
  • Upgrade proven towers instead of panic-building new ones.
  • Protect lives to preserve bonus score and future momentum.

Economy — upgrade efficiency wins long runs

Overspending on new towers too early is the classic mistake. Upgrades often create more value because they add damage to positions that already work. Expand only when coverage breaks down or a new route segment matters. In Endless, efficient gold conversion matters even more: stable waves create safer score submissions, better bonus accumulation, and room to exploit aura synergies later.

Endless — how to survive longer

Long runs are about discipline: keep a clean frontline, avoid unnecessary leaks, and treat every spell as a recovery tool for pressure spikes. If your layout only works while spells are up, it's too fragile. The simplest endless plan wins: stack reliable damage first, add splash for control, layer in Cryo's Brittle to amplify everything, and reinforce boss answers before greed takes over.
