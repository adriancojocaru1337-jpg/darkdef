Codex

Field manual for towers, enemies, and boss threats

The Dark Defense codex is built for quick tactical reference. Learn which towers solve which waves, what each spell is best at, and how every boss tries to break your line.

Battle Doctrine

How to use this codex

  • Use Archer and Hunter to stabilize priority targets and early leaks.
  • Use Mage and Bomb Tower when the route creates natural choke points.
  • Save Frost Nova to lock enemies inside a kill zone, not just to buy random time.
  • Respect boss waves: each boss has a named ability and changes how you should place towers.
  • In Endless Mode, balance safety and greed. Surviving one more wave matters more than a flashy build.

Tower Codex

Core towers and what they are really for

Archer Starter

Archer

Cheap, flexible, and perfect for opening waves. Best when you need reliable coverage without overcommitting gold.

  • Cost: 90
  • Best use: early coverage, anti-leak support, efficient filler around corners
  • Weakness: falls off if you ask it to solve armored pressure alone
  • Specs: Rapid Shot for fast enemies, Heavy Arrows for tougher fronts
Hunter Single Target

Hunter

High range and stronger hits make Hunter your answer to elite enemies and bosses that need focused damage.

  • Cost: 190
  • Best use: boss lanes, priority targets, long-route maps
  • Weakness: inefficient into large clustered waves without splash support
  • Specs: Sniper for reach and burst, Tracker for anti-fast pressure
Mage Splash

Mage

Magic splash tower for packed lanes. Mage shines when enemies overlap and your single-target towers start wasting shots.

  • Cost: 235
  • Best use: clustered enemies, mid-game wave control, choke points
  • Weakness: less efficient when enemies are spread out
  • Specs: Frost Mage for control, Arc Mage for heavier area damage
Bomb Burst AoE

Bomb Tower

Heavy splash burst that punishes dense waves and armored clusters. Strongest when the path forces enemies to stack.

  • Cost: 305
  • Best use: choke points, armored windows, damage spikes before leaks
  • Weakness: expensive to rush too early
  • Specs: Siege for raw impact, Shock for more disruptive wave control
Spell Codex

Three spells, three different jobs

Frost Nova 18s CD

Large slow field. Best used when a dangerous pack is entering your main tower cluster.

Meteor Strike 24s CD

Reliable area damage. Strong against grouped waves and boss escort packs that are about to break through.

Chain Lightning 16s CD

Fast cleanup tool that jumps between nearby enemies. Excellent for stabilizing messy fronts and finishing runners.

Enemy Codex

Know the wave before it reaches the gate

Normal Baseline

Standard pressure unit. They define whether your build is efficient or merely flashy.

  • Use them to judge if your opening is stable.
  • If normals leak, your economy or placement is off.
Fast Leak Risk

Low durability, high speed. They punish greedy builds and weak corner coverage.

  • Use Rapid Archer, Tracker Hunter, or Frost Nova.
  • Late-stage fast waves can snowball into instant panic.
Tank Attrition

Slower but much harder to kill. Tanks expose weak single-target damage and overreliance on cheap towers.

  • Hunters and Heavy Arrow Archers help a lot.
  • Do not let tanks absorb all your splash while fast enemies slip by.
Armored Wave Spike

Appears in heavier wave windows and demands burst plus enough total coverage behind it.

  • Bomb Towers are strongest when armored enemies bunch together.
  • Backline support still matters because armored units often screen faster threats.
Splitter Endless Only

Appears in Endless and breaks into two shardlings on death. It punishes overkill and weak cleanup coverage.

  • Bomb Towers, mages, and chain damage help clean up the split.
  • If shardlings leak, your backline cleanup is too thin.
Boss Codex

Stage bosses and their signature abilities

Stage 1 Root Grasp

Ancient Treant Skull

Snare-based boss. It punishes overreliance on a single carry tower by temporarily disabling key positions.

Stage 2 Blood Rage

Ruin Warden

Gains a dangerous power surge. Prepare burst damage or a control spell before it hits the middle of the route.

Stage 3 Grave Summons

Grave Necromancer

Spawns fast minions while pressuring the lane itself. Splash plus anti-fast support is mandatory here.

Stage 4 Void Bulwark

Iron Castellan

Protective shield boss. If your burst is badly timed, it can walk through your best damage window almost untouched.

Stage 5 Blood Rage

Catacomb Devourer

An even harsher rage variant. This is where weak scaling and sloppy spell timing start to collapse entire runs.

Stage 6 Void Bulwark

Dark Portal Overlord

The campaign capstone. Expect huge pressure, shielding, and far less room for inefficient placements.

Tactical Advice

Best habits before you start a serious run

  • Build for the path, not just for tower fantasy. Choke points are where splash earns its gold.
  • Never let your entire defense depend on one tower surviving or one spell being off cooldown.
  • Use early-game efficiency to afford cleaner mid-game scaling, not to hoard gold forever.
  • Before every boss wave, ask: do I need more burst, more control, or more anti-fast backup?
