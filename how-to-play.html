How to Play

Beginner guide for Dark Defense

This guide explains the basics of tower placement, upgrades, spell timing, and survival priorities so new players can reach later stages faster and build stronger endless runs.

Core Loop

What to do first

  1. Place early towers near corners and long path segments for maximum attack time.
  2. Start waves only after spending your opening gold efficiently.
  3. Upgrade your highest-impact towers instead of spreading resources too thin.
  4. Use spells reactively when leaks are likely, not the moment they come off cooldown.
  5. Prepare specifically for boss waves with stronger single-target coverage.
Tower Roles

Quick unit breakdown

  • Archer: cheap and reliable early-game tower
  • Hunter: high range and better single-target pressure
  • Mage: splash damage for clustered enemies
  • Bomb Tower: heavy burst for dense waves and choke points
Spell Usage

When to cast each spell

  • Frost Nova: best for slowing a dense pack entering a kill zone.
  • Meteor Strike: strongest when multiple enemies overlap or a boss is supported by minions.
  • Chain Lightning: useful when several enemies are close and you need instant cleanup.

Holding spells for danger moments is usually stronger than using them on cooldown. The best players treat spells as tools to prevent leaks, stabilize bad waves, and protect score potential.

Positioning

Use route geometry, not random placement

The strongest layouts are built around repeated exposure. Place towers where enemies stay in range the longest, especially around turns, long lanes, and overlapping coverage pockets. A modest tower in the right tile usually outperforms a stronger tower placed badly.

Try to build layered kill zones. Let splash towers soften groups while longer-range towers finish priority targets before they reach the final third of the path.

Boss Prep

Plan waves before they start

  • Save gold before boss waves instead of making weak filler purchases.
  • Favor strong single-target uptime for boss encounters.
  • Keep at least one spell ready as insurance.
  • Upgrade proven towers instead of panic-building in bad spots.
  • Protect lives to preserve momentum and cleaner score runs.
Economy

Upgrade efficiency wins longer runs

A common mistake is overspending on fresh towers too early. Upgrades often create more value because they improve positions that are already performing. Expand only when your coverage is clearly breaking down or a new route segment becomes strategically important.

If a tower placement is already excellent, investing there tends to beat scattering weak towers across the map.

Endless Mode

How to survive longer

Long endless runs depend on discipline. Maintain a clean frontline, avoid greedy leaks, and treat every spell as a recovery tool for pressure spikes. If your defense only works while spells are available, the build is too fragile.

The safest pattern is reliable damage first, splash second, and boss answers before greed takes over.

Practical Goals

What strong runs usually get right

  1. Open with efficient towers instead of saving too much gold.
  2. Build one real kill zone rather than several weak ones.
  3. Upgrade the best positions before adding expensive extras.
  4. Respect fast waves with control, not panic.
  5. Enter boss waves with a plan for burst damage and one backup spell.
