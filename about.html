The Project

About Dark Defense

A dark fantasy tower defense that runs instantly in the browser — no installs, no launchers, no waiting. Six stages, six bosses, and an Endless mode that never stops scaling.

What it is

Every wave asks for a better decision: where to place towers, when to spend on upgrades instead of expanding, and when to hold a spell rather than cast it. The mechanics stay readable, but the pressure keeps rising.

Campaign stages each introduce a new pressure point and end with a boss that has its own ability to work around. Clear all six and Endless opens — unlimited waves, doubled enemy health, and boss pairs every tenth wave. That's where leaderboard runs are made.

Sessions are short by design, but nothing is lost when a run ends: every attempt pays out Crystals toward permanent upgrades.

What's in it

  • Six stages with distinct routes, art and boss encounters.
  • Five towers, each with two specializations that fork its role.
  • Three spells — Frost Nova, Meteor Strike, Chain Lightning.
  • Ascension — permanent upgrades bought with Crystals earned every run.
  • Legendary auras drafted after boss kills for run-defining builds.
  • Endless Mode with a global bonus-score leaderboard.
  • Daily challenge — a new modifier every midnight, with its own ladder.
  • Verified scores — server-side run validation on submission.

Why it exists

Most browser games ask you to accept less: thinner mechanics, weaker art, progress that vanishes when you close the tab. Dark Defense was built to test whether a web game could feel complete instead — something you launch in seconds, understand in a minute, and still be improving at weeks later.

That meant meta-progression that survives between sessions, bosses with real abilities rather than bigger health bars, and a leaderboard worth defending. It runs on plain HTML5 and Canvas, with accounts and score validation handled server-side.
