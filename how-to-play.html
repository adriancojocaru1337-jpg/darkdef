How to Play

Beginner guide for Dark Defense

This guide explains the basics of tower placement, upgrades, spell timing, and survival priorities so new players can reach later stages faster and build stronger endless runs.

Core Loop

What to do first

  1. Place early towers near corners and long path segments for maximum attack time.
  2. Start waves only after spending your opening gold efficiently.
  3. Upgrade your highest-impact towers instead of spreading resources too thin.
  4. Use spells reactively when leaks are likely, not the moment they come off cooldown.
  5. Prepare specifically for boss waves with stronger single-target coverage.
Tower Roles

Quick unit breakdown

  • Archer: cheap and reliable early-game tower
  • Hunter: high range and better single-target pressure
  • Mage: splash damage for clustered enemies
  • Bomb Tower: heavy burst for dense waves and choke points
  • Cryo: permanently unlock for 150 Crystals in Ascension, then place for 160 gold. Only one Cryo can be active at a time (sell it to place another). Its chill field slows enemies and its shots stack Brittle for up to +40% damage received — and it has its own permanent Ascension upgrades for chill, range, damage and Brittle.
Tower Synergies

Bonuses for adjacent towers

Every tower grants a bonus to towers of a different type placed in one of the 8 cells around it. Each neighbor type counts only once, so duplicates do not stack.

  • 🏹 Archer nearby — Volley Drill: +8% attack speed
  • 🎯 Hunter nearby — Marked Targets: +8% damage
  • 🔮 Mage nearby — Arcane Insight: +6% range
  • 💣 Bomb nearby — Shrapnel Craft: +10% splash radius (splash towers only)
  • ❄ Cryo nearby — Cryo Fracture: +12% damage to adjacent non-Cryo towers

While placing a tower, dotted lines show which neighbors it will link with. Select a placed tower to see its active synergy links. Clustered builds earn strong bonuses but cover less of the path — spreading out trades power for coverage.

Combos & New Threats

Kill streaks and the Splitter

  • Combo: every kill within 2.6 seconds of the last one extends your streak. At x5 kills score is multiplied by 1.25, at x10 by 1.5, and at x20 by 2. Finishing a x10+ streak grants bonus score — but a single enemy reaching the gate breaks the combo with no reward.
  • Splitter: from Stage 3 onward, this teal blob splits into two smaller, faster fragments when killed. Splash towers handle it best — pure single-target builds will struggle.

Combos reward builds that keep kills flowing constantly. Slows and splash coverage help bridge the gaps between enemy packs and protect your streak.

Permanent Progression

Ascension & Crystals

Every run — win or lose — earns Crystals: a little from each wave cleared, more from clearing stages and defeating bosses. Spend them in Ascension on permanent upgrades that carry into every future run, so you grow stronger the more you play. Progress is saved locally and synced to your account when you are signed in.

  • 🔆 Radiance: raw firepower — more tower damage, faster attacks, extra range, and empowered ley stones.
  • 🛡️ Bastion: economy and survival — bonus starting gold and lives, more gold from kills, and stronger stage-clear healing.
  • 🔮 Arcana: spells and drafts — shorter spell cooldowns, aura-draft rerolls, more Crystals earned, and wider boss aura choices.
  • ❄ Special Towers: unlock the Cryo Tower (150 Crystals), then invest in its four permanent upgrades — Deep Frost (stronger chill), Glacial Reach (range & field), Frostbite (damage) and Shatterpoint (extra Brittle per hit), each with four ranks.

Node costs rise with each rank, so early attunements are cheap catch-up power while the deepest upgrades — especially the Cryo tree — are long-term goals worth grinding toward.

Spell Usage

When to cast each spell

  • Frost Nova: best for slowing a dense pack entering a kill zone.
  • Meteor Strike: strongest when multiple enemies overlap or a boss is supported by minions.
  • Chain Lightning: useful when several enemies are close and you need instant cleanup.

Holding spells for danger moments is usually stronger than using them on cooldown. The best players treat spells as tools to prevent leaks, stabilize bad waves, and protect score potential.
