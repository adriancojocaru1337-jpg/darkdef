Boss Doctrine

Beating every boss

Six bosses, four abilities, and a specific answer to each. What to build before the fight, what to hold, and how the rules change when Endless starts sending them in pairs.

The four abilities

Six bosses share four abilities between them, so learning the counters once covers the whole campaign. What changes is the health pool and how little room for error you get.

Stage 1 Ancient Treant Skull
Root Grasp — disables one of your towers

The danger isn't the damage, it's the hole. If your whole defense sits in one pocket, losing a tower opens the lane. Cover every segment with at least two towers before this fight, even if both are cheap Archers. Redundancy beats power here.

Stage 2 Ruin Warden
Blood Rage — sudden burst of speed

It tries to cross your kill zone before you've done enough damage. Hold Frost Nova until the surge starts — casting early wastes it entirely. A Frost Mage or Cryo covering the back half is insurance if your timing slips.

Stage 3 Grave Necromancer
Grave Summons — spawns minions mid-fight

Single-target builds drown here: your damage is locked on the boss while adds walk past. Bring at least one Mage or Bomb before this stage. Splash clears the adds without ever taking damage off the boss.

Stage 4 Iron Castellan
Void Bulwark — absorbs damage behind a shell

Damage poured into the shield is wasted. Stop casting when it goes up and let sustained tower fire wear it down instead. Save Meteor Strike for the moment it drops — burst into an unshielded boss is worth several times the same spell used early.

Stage 5 Catacomb Devourer
Blood Rage — faster and deadlier than stage 2

Same ability, far less forgiveness. One slow spell is no longer enough. Build layered kill zones — two separate pockets rather than one strong cluster — so the surge still passes through damage after clearing the first.

Stage 6 Dark Portal Overlord
Void Bulwark — shields at the worst moments

The final boss times its shell to blunt your strongest windows. Maximum single-target uptime wins this: upgraded Hunters with Sniper, plus a maxed Bomb. Enter with every spell available and spend them only in the gaps between shields.

Before every boss

  • Bank gold through the last waves. Filler towers bought at wave 9 rarely matter; the upgrade you can afford at wave 10 does.
  • Upgrade, don't expand. Bosses are a single high-health target — concentrated damage beats spread coverage.
  • Enter with at least one spell ready. Treat it as insurance, not as damage you're saving up.
  • Check your Cryo coverage. Brittle stacks amplify every other tower — it's the cheapest damage boost available.
  • Protect your lives. They carry into your bonus score and your combo multiplier.

Endless boss pairs

From Endless wave 10, bosses arrive two at a time — and now escorted by a full horde rather than alone. Every cycle they get tougher.

  • Clear the escort first. Splash damage on the horde, then focus the pair — leaking mobs while you burn a boss is how runs end.
  • Two abilities fire at once. A shield plus a speed surge in the same window means you can't answer both — answer the surge, outlast the shield.
  • Control scales, damage doesn't. Enemy health doubles and keeps climbing; slows and Brittle stay valuable forever.
  • Don't overreach after a clear. The wave after a boss pair is your rebuild window — reinforce before you expand.
